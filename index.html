Tour Mysore Today!

Mysore, officially known as Mysuru, is one of the most beautiful and historic cities in Karnataka. It is famous for its rich cultural heritage, magnificent palaces, and traditional festivals. The city is especially known for the grand Mysore Palace, which attracts thousands of tourists every year with its stunning architecture and dazzling illumination during festivals. Mysore is also called the “City of Palaces” and is popular for its silk sarees, sandalwood products, and incense sticks. The Chamundi Hills, located near the city, provide a scenic view and are home to the famous Chamundeshwari Temple. The world-famous Mysore Dasara festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm, showcasing the royal heritage and cultural traditions of the region. With its clean streets, pleasant climate, and historical importance, Mysore is one of the top tourist destinations in South India.

St. Philomena’s Church

St. Philomena's Church in Mysore is one of the most magnificent churches in India. Built in 1936, it is designed in Neo-Gothic style, inspired by the famous Cologne Cathedral in Germany. The church is dedicated to Saint Philomena, a Roman Catholic saint and martyr. It is known for its tall twin spires, beautiful stained-glass windows, and peaceful atmosphere. The colorful glass panels inside the church depict important scenes from the life of Jesus Christ. The church can accommodate around 800 people and attracts visitors from different religions because of its grand architecture and calm surroundings. Located near the Mysore Palace, it is one of the main tourist attractions in Mysuru.

VenuGopalaSwami Betta

Venugopalaswamy Betta is a beautiful hilltop temple located near Krishna Raja Sagar (KRS) in Mysuru district, Karnataka. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna in the form of Venugopala (Krishna playing the flute). It was originally built in the 12th century during the Hoysala period and was later relocated and restored because the old temple site was submerged due to the KRS dam. The temple stands on a small hill (betta) surrounded by the scenic backwaters of KRS, offering a peaceful and breathtaking view, especially during sunrise and sunset. The architecture reflects traditional Hoysala style with detailed carvings and stone structures. Today, Venugopalaswamy Betta is a popular tourist spot for photography, sightseeing, and spiritual visits. Its calm environment and natural beauty make it one of the hidden gems near Mysuru.

Rail Museum

The Railway Museum in Mysore is one of the most interesting attractions for history and train lovers. It was established in 1979 by Indian Railways and is the second railway museum in India after Delhi. The museum displays vintage steam locomotives, royal coaches, old railway equipment, and photographs that show the development of railways in India. One of the main attractions is the Maharani’s Saloon Coach, which was used by the royal family of Mysore. Visitors can also see antique signals, telegraph instruments, and different types of railway engines. The museum has a small toy train ride that is especially popular among children.
